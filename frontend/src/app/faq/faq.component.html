Greywater is wastewater from household activities that do not involve human waste. It typically comes from showers, baths, washing machines, and hand basins. Greywater is less contaminated than blackwater, which comes from toilets, kitchen sinks, and floor drains. Greywater can be reused for non-potable purposes, such as watering plants, flushing toilets, and washing clothes.

Here is a table that summarizes the key differences between Greywater and Blackwater:
Caractéristique Eau grise Eau noire
Source Showers, baths, washing machines, hand basins, kitchen sinks Toilets, floor drains
Contaminants Low levels of bacteria and organic matter High levels of bacteria, viruses, and other pathogens
Réutilisation Can be reused for non-potable purposes Cannot be reused

Certifications

The 7-Layer Ceramic Water Purifier Filter has CE approval. This means that it has met the European Union's standards for safety and performance. The Dracoss Greywater Recycling Machine is currently undergoing NSF certification. NSF certification is a rigorous process that ensures that a product meets the highest standards for safety and performance.

CE and NSF certification are important because they give you peace of mind knowing that the products you are using are safe and effective. CE certification is especially important for products that are sold in the European Union. NSF certification is also important for products that are used in food and beverage processing.

NSF-350 is a standard developed by the NSF International, a non-profit organization that sets standards for food safety, water quality, and other products. NSF-350 establishes requirements for the materials, design, construction, and performance of onsite residential and commercial water reuse treatment systems. It also sets water quality requirements for the reduction of chemical and microbiological contaminants for non-potable water use.
